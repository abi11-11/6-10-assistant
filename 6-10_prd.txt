# Product Requirements Document: 6:10 Assistant

## 1. Executive Summary & Problem Statement

### 1.1 Problem Statement
Industrial facilities like the Ridgeway Site operate around the clock. Overnight, these sites generate signals such as fence alerts, vehicle paths, and badge failures. By morning, it is clear something occurred, but the full narrative remains unknown. Maya, the operations lead, arrives at 6:10 AM and has less than two hours to separate noise from genuine threats. She does not want overwhelming dashboards or a chatbot making confident guesses.

### 1.2 Product Vision
The 6:10 Assistant is an AI-first, hosted product designed for decision-makers managing judgment under uncertainty. It bridges the gap between raw overnight signals and a trusted morning decision, helping the user uncover the truth faster.

### 1.3 Core Workflow
The product converts overnight signals into evidence-backed hypotheses, validates them through tools and spatial reasoning, proposes follow-up investigation when uncertainty remains, and submits findings for human approval before generating a briefing.

### 1.4 Primary Morning Workspace
The primary user workspace shall be a focused morning investigation view composed of a prioritized findings queue, a map or spatial panel, an evidence and timeline panel, and explicit human review actions for approval, rejection, and refinement.

### 1.5 Key Objectives
* **O1:** Help Maya reach a credible understanding of overnight activity before the 8:00 AM review.
* **O2:** Make follow-up investigation visible, structured, and easy to challenge.
* **O3:** Produce a leadership-ready morning briefing grounded in reviewable evidence.

### 1.6 Success Criteria (SMART)
* **SC-01:** In seeded test scenarios, the system shall produce a draft morning summary by 8:00 AM equivalent workflow time for 100% of overnight incident datasets.
* **SC-02:** In seeded test scenarios, Maya shall be able to reach a first evidence-backed hypothesis within 10 minutes of opening the morning workflow.
* **SC-03:** For 100% of simulated drone follow-ups, the system shall display the proposed route, investigation targets, expected checks, and rationale before the user approves execution.
* **SC-04:** For 100% of AI-generated findings included in the final briefing workflow, the system shall require an explicit reviewer action of approve, reject, or refine before publication.
* **SC-05:** In seeded test scenarios, the system shall reduce raw overnight events into a prioritized review queue of no more than 5 findings per scenario before human review.

## 2. Target Audience & Personas

* **Primary User: Maya (Operations Lead)**
    * *Context:* Arrives at 6:10 AM with less than two hours before the morning review. Needs a system that helps her get to the truth faster rather than a flood of logs.
* **Secondary User: Nisha (Site Head)**
    * *Context:* Walks in at 8:00 AM asking what happened, what matters, and what she needs to care about.
* **Secondary User: Raghav (Night Supervisor)**
    * *Context:* Signs off before the morning review but leaves behind handoff notes for the ops team.

---

## 3. Product Philosophy & Design Principles

* **P1 (AI-First Workflow):** The system investigates and gathers context before the user manually does everything. The AI must do real work, such as using tools and forming a draft understanding.
* **P2 (Honest Uncertainty):** The system must surface uncertainty honestly rather than making confident guesses.
* **P3 (Human Authority):** Humans must stay in control. The interface must allow the user to inspect, challenge, refine, or approve the AI's output.

---

## 4. User Journeys

### 4.1 Maya Reviews the Overnight Narrative
1. Maya opens the system at 6:10 AM and sees the overnight incidents already clustered into a draft narrative.
2. The agent highlights what likely happened, what evidence supports it, and what remains uncertain.
3. Maya inspects the map, timeline, and evidence links to confirm whether the narrative is credible.
4. Maya marks findings as approved, rejected, or needing follow-up.

### 4.2 Maya Requests a Follow-Up Check
1. Maya spots an unresolved or suspicious event in the draft findings.
2. The system proposes a lightweight drone patrol or follow-up mission with route, checkpoints, and rationale.
3. Maya reviews the simulated mission on the spatial interface and adjusts or approves it.
4. The system incorporates the follow-up outcome into the working narrative.

### 4.3 Nisha Consumes the Morning Briefing
1. Nisha opens the approved morning briefing before or during the 8:00 AM review.
2. She sees what happened, what was harmless, what needs escalation, and what remains uncertain.
3. She can trace each key conclusion back to reviewed evidence and the final human decision.

## 5. Product Scope

### 5.1 MVP / In Scope
* Overnight incident ingestion from seeded or simulated data sources.
* AI-generated draft findings that summarize likely events, supporting evidence, open questions, and recommended next actions.
* A primary morning investigation workspace with findings queue, spatial panel, evidence timeline, and review actions.
* At least one MCP-backed or MCP-style investigation tool used during live workflow execution.
* Lightweight drone follow-up mission simulation used to resolve investigation uncertainty.
* Human review, approval, rejection, and refinement before briefing publication.
* Morning briefing generation based on reviewed and approved findings.

### 5.2 Out of Scope
* Real drone integration.
* Live video feeds.
* Image processing.
* 3D mapping.
* Actual sensor integrations.
* Physics-heavy flight simulation.
* Multiplayer collaboration.

### 5.3 Later / Growth
* Additional investigation tools beyond the first MCP-backed tool.
* Richer simulation behaviors and more advanced mission planning.
* Expanded reporting views for leadership and handoff use cases.

## 6. Functional Requirements (FRs)

**The following requirements are strictly NON-NEGOTIABLE and represent the core evaluation criteria for the system.**

* **FR-01: AI-First Investigation Workflow [NON-NEGOTIABLE]**
    * The system MUST generate a draft overnight narrative before manual review by correlating seeded incident data into findings, open questions, supporting evidence references, and recommended next actions for every seeded overnight scenario.
* **FR-02: Agentic Tool Usage [NON-NEGOTIABLE]**
    * The system MUST incorporate an agent that decides which investigation tool or tools to use dynamically based on the current investigation state and unresolved questions. For each seeded investigation scenario, the agent shall invoke tools whenever the current hypothesis lacks enough evidence to classify an event as harmless, suspicious, or escalatable.
* **FR-03: MCP Interface Implementation [NON-NEGOTIABLE]**
    * At least one investigation tool MUST be invoked through the required MCP (Model Context Protocol) or an MCP-style interface as part of the live workflow. That integration shall return evidence objects containing event identifier, zone, time window, and confidence metadata for use in the investigation workflow.
* **FR-04: Spatial Workflow Integration [NON-NEGOTIABLE]**
    * A map or spatial interface MUST allow the user to inspect event locations, movement paths, zones of interest, and follow-up mission coverage as part of the core investigation workflow for every prioritized finding.
* **FR-05: Drone Mission Simulation [NON-NEGOTIABLE]**
    * The system MUST provide a drone patrol or follow-up mission simulation that shows route, checkpoints, the purpose of each check, and the uncertainty being resolved before human approval.
* **FR-06: Human Review & Governance Layer [NON-NEGOTIABLE]**
    * The system MUST feature a review layer where the human can inspect supporting evidence, override findings, refine conclusions, and approve or reject outputs before publication for every finding included in the final briefing workflow.
* **FR-07: Briefing Generation**
    * The system shall produce a morning summary, handoff, or briefing that includes approved findings, unresolved uncertainties, and recommended actions based only on reviewed and approved investigations.

## 7. Non-Functional Requirements (NFRs)

* **NFR-01 (Data Sourcing):** The system shall rely entirely on seeded or simulated data for 100% of demo and validation scenarios, as verified by static test fixtures and scenario configuration.
* **NFR-02 (Workspace Load Time):** The primary morning workspace shall load its initial findings queue, map panel, and evidence summary in under 3 seconds for the seeded demo dataset on the hosted target environment.
* **NFR-03 (Accessibility):** The web application shall meet WCAG 2.1 AA for core morning workflow screens, including findings review, map interaction controls, and briefing approval actions, as verified by accessibility audit and keyboard-only review of the seeded demo workflow.
* **NFR-04 (Clarity Under Time Pressure):** The system shall present no more than 5 prioritized findings in the initial review queue for the seeded demo dataset, with each finding displaying status, confidence, and recommended action.

---

## 8. Web App Requirements

* **Browser Support:** The application shall support the latest two stable versions of Chrome, Edge, and Firefox on desktop.
* **Responsive Design:** The primary experience is optimized for desktop review from 1280px width and above; below that width, the system shall preserve access to findings review and approval actions without hiding core information.
* **Performance Targets:** User-triggered transitions between findings, map focus updates, and evidence panel updates shall complete in under 500ms for the seeded demo dataset.
* **Accessibility Level:** Core morning workflow screens shall conform to WCAG 2.1 AA.
* **SEO Strategy:** SEO is not applicable for the initial release because the product is intended as a private operations workflow rather than a public acquisition surface.

## 10. Epic & Story Decomposition

### 10.1 Epic Framing Principles

The epics are organized around distinct user-value increments rather than internal implementation layers. Each epic must enable a meaningful user decision that can be validated independently during MVP delivery.

* **Epic 1 decision enabled:** Maya can understand what likely happened overnight and where to look first.
* **Epic 2 decision enabled:** Maya can increase or decrease confidence in an initial explanation without manually hunting through systems.
* **Epic 3 decision enabled:** Maya can decide whether remote evidence is insufficient and an operational follow-up should be proposed.
* **Epic 4 decision enabled:** Maya can approve, adjust, or reject the final judgment before it is communicated to leadership.

### 10.2 Cross-Epic Domain Contracts

The following domain objects shall be defined before story creation so later epics extend a stable investigation model rather than invent parallel structures:

* **Finding:** A prioritized incident statement with status, confidence, rationale, recommended next action, and linked evidence references.
* **Evidence:** A referenced observation with source, event identifier, zone, time window, confidence metadata, and provenance.
* **Hypothesis:** A candidate explanation that connects one or more findings and evidence items, including what is still uncertain.
* **Mission:** A simulated follow-up investigation proposal with route, checkpoints, target uncertainty, expected checks, and predicted evidence return.
* **Decision:** A human-reviewed outcome for a finding or hypothesis, including approve, reject, or refine actions and reviewer rationale.
* **Briefing:** A publication-ready summary composed only of reviewed decisions, approved findings, unresolved uncertainties, and recommended actions.

### 10.3 Epic List

* **Epic 1: Morning Incident Review**
    * User value: Maya quickly orients to overnight incidents through a prioritized review workspace with findings, map context, and timeline evidence.
    * Boundary: This epic is limited to seeded baseline investigation and reviewable presentation of the initial narrative.
    * Story 1.1: Develop the AI-first hypothesis engine to convert overnight data into prioritized findings, open questions, supporting evidence references, and recommended next actions. *(Satisfies FR-01)*
    * Story 1.2: Develop the morning investigation workspace with findings queue, map focus, and evidence timeline for seeded scenarios. *(Satisfies FR-04)*
* **Epic 2: Confidence Refinement Through Evidence Expansion**
    * User value: Maya can strengthen, falsify, or clarify an initial explanation through targeted digital evidence gathering rather than manual system hunting.
    * Boundary: This epic covers remote or digital evidence enrichment only and must reuse the canonical finding, evidence, and hypothesis models from Epic 1.
    * Story 2.1: Build the agent routing logic that detects unresolved questions and decides when additional evidence is needed. *(Satisfies FR-02)*
    * Story 2.2: Implement the MCP-compliant interface and register at least one functioning investigation tool that returns evidence objects into the shared investigation model. *(Satisfies FR-03)*
* **Epic 3: Operational Follow-Up for Unresolved Uncertainty**
    * User value: Maya can review a proposed operational intervention when remote evidence is insufficient to resolve a suspicious or ambiguous finding.
    * Boundary: This epic starts only when digital evidence is insufficient and is limited to simulated mission proposal, review, and predicted evidence return.
    * Story 3.1: Build the lightweight drone patrol simulation overlay with route, checkpoints, purpose of each check, and uncertainty being resolved. *(Satisfies FR-05)*
* **Epic 4: Reviewed Decisions and Morning Briefing**
    * User value: Maya retains final authority over the assistant's judgment and Nisha receives a traceable, approval-backed morning briefing.
    * Boundary: Reviewability and provenance must exist earlier, but this epic introduces formal approval workflow and briefing publication.
    * Story 4.1: Create the human-in-the-loop review workflow for Maya to inspect evidence, override findings, refine conclusions, and approve or reject outputs. *(Satisfies FR-06)*
    * Story 4.2: Generate the final morning briefing from reviewed decisions, approved findings, unresolved uncertainties, and recommended actions. *(Satisfies FR-07)*
